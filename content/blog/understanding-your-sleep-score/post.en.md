## What Is the Sleep Score?

![Indexo app home dashboard](/images/product/app-home-dashboard.png)

Every morning, the Indexo app greets you with a single number: your **Sleep Score**. Ranging from 0 to 100, this composite metric distills a night of complex physiological data into one clear indicator of how well you slept.

But what goes into that number? And more importantly, how should you use it? Let us break it down.

## The Five Pillars of Your Score

Your Sleep Score is calculated from five weighted components, each reflecting a different dimension of sleep quality:

### 1. Sleep Duration (25%)

How long you actually slept — not just time in bed, but time in verified sleep stages. The target varies by age and individual, but generally falls between 7 and 9 hours for adults.

### 2. Sleep Efficiency (20%)

The ratio of time asleep to total time in bed. An efficiency above 85% is considered good; above 90% is excellent. Low efficiency often signals difficulty falling asleep or frequent wake-ups.

### 3. Deep Sleep Percentage (25%)

![Indexo app sleep stage analysis](/images/product/app-sleep.png)

Deep sleep (also called slow-wave sleep) is when your body does its heaviest repair work — tissue regeneration, immune strengthening, and memory consolidation. Most adults need 15–25% of their total sleep time in deep stages.

The Indexo system detects deep sleep through characteristic changes in heart rate and respiratory patterns that differ from lighter sleep phases.

### 4. Heart Rate Variability Trend (15%)

![Real-time vital signs monitoring](/images/product/app-vitals.png)

HRV measures the variation in time between consecutive heartbeats. Higher HRV during sleep generally indicates better autonomic nervous system recovery. Rather than using a single-night value, the Indexo algorithm compares your nightly HRV against your personal 30-day rolling baseline.

- **Above baseline**: Your body recovered well — positive score contribution
- **At baseline**: Normal recovery — neutral contribution
- **Below baseline**: Suboptimal recovery — reduced score

### 5. Restlessness Index (15%)

Movement during sleep is normal, but excessive restlessness fragments sleep architecture and reduces restorative value. The Indexo sensor tracks micro-movements throughout the night.

## Your Personal Baseline: Why It Matters

One of the most powerful aspects of the Indexo system is **personalization**. Rather than comparing you to population averages, the algorithm builds a model specific to your body over the first 14 days of use.

This means a resting heart rate of 55 bpm might be excellent for you but concerning for someone else. Your ideal deep sleep percentage might differ from textbook averages. After the initial learning period, your Sleep Score reflects *your* normal, not a generic standard.

## Recovery: The Other Side of the Coin

![Recovery score tracking](/images/product/app-recovery.png)

Your Sleep Score tells you about the quality of your sleep itself, but the **Recovery Score** tells you about its *effect* on your body. Think of it this way: sleep quality is the input, and recovery is the output.

The Recovery screen analyzes your cardiovascular recovery during sleep, including heart rate dipping patterns and HRV restoration. Together, Sleep Score and Recovery Score give you a complete picture of your nighttime health.

## Trends Matter More Than Single Nights

![Sleep regularity tracking](/images/product/app-regularity.png)

A single night's score can be influenced by many factors: a late meal, a stressful day, travel, or simply sleeping in an unfamiliar environment. That is why the Indexo app emphasizes **trend analysis** over isolated readings.

The Weekly and Monthly trend views reveal patterns that single-night scores cannot:

- Is your deep sleep declining over weeks? This might signal overtraining, chronic stress, or a sleep environment issue.
- Is your HRV trending upward? Great — your body is adapting positively to recent lifestyle changes.
- Do your lowest scores cluster around certain days? You may discover that specific habits consistently affect your sleep.

## Practical Tips to Improve Your Score

Based on the data patterns we see across our user base, here are actionable steps that tend to yield the most improvement:

### Consistency Is King

Going to bed and waking up at roughly the same time — even on weekends — is the single most impactful habit for sleep quality.

### Optimize Your Environment

- Keep your bedroom cool (18–20°C is ideal for most people)
- Minimize light exposure — blackout curtains or a sleep mask help
- Reduce noise disruptions — white noise or earplugs if needed

### Wind Down Before Bed

Avoid screens, intense exercise, and heavy meals within 90 minutes of bedtime.

### Monitor, Don't Obsess

Your Sleep Score is a tool for awareness, not a grade to stress over. Some nights will be lower than others — that is completely normal. Focus on the weekly trend, not the daily number.
