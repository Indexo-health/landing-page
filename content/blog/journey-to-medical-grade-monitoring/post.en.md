## A Problem Hiding in Plain Sight

Sleep disorders affect an estimated one billion people worldwide. Among them, obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) alone impacts roughly 936 million adults globally — yet **up to 80% remain undiagnosed**. These are not edge cases. These are our parents, partners, and friends, going through life with a treatable condition that silently damages their cardiovascular health, cognitive function, and quality of life.

The gap between the scale of the problem and the accessibility of solutions is staggering. Clinical polysomnography — the gold standard for sleep diagnosis — requires an overnight hospital stay, costs hundreds to thousands of dollars, and has wait times stretching months in many regions.

**We founded Indexo Health to close that gap.**

## The Mission: From Reactive to Proactive

![Indexo app AI care insights](/images/product/app-ai-care.png)

Healthcare has long been reactive. We wait for symptoms to appear, then scramble for answers. But sleep health is uniquely suited for a proactive approach: it happens every single night, in the same location, following predictable physiological patterns.

What if we could monitor those patterns continuously — not for one night in a hospital, but for every night at home? What if we could catch the earliest signs of trouble before they become clinical problems?

This is the core of the Indexo mission: **shifting sleep health from episodic diagnosis to continuous, preventive monitoring.**

## Why We Chose Fiber-Optic BCG

![Fiber-optic sensing diagram](/images/technology/fiber-optics-diagram.png)

When we set out to build a home sleep monitor, we evaluated dozens of sensing technologies. Accelerometers, piezoelectric films, radar-based systems, and camera arrays all had merit — and all had fundamental limitations.

We needed a technology that was:

1. **Sensitive enough** to capture heartbeat-level micro-vibrations through a mattress
2. **Stable over time** — no drift, no degradation, no recalibration
3. **Immune to interference** — phones on the nightstand, Wi-Fi routers, electric blankets
4. **Completely passive** — no emissions, no radiation, nothing the sleeper could feel

Fiber-optic BCG checked every box. By threading ultra-thin optical fibers through a flexible mat, we created a sensor that detects the mechanical impulse of each heartbeat as it propagates through the body and into the mattress surface.

![Clinical study results — Indexo BCG vs. hospital-grade reference](/images/technology/clinical-study-results.png)

Our clinical validation studies have shown strong correlation with gold-standard hospital equipment, confirming that our home-based approach delivers the accuracy that matters.

## Building the Product

![The complete Indexo CS-03 system](/images/product/cs03-components.png)

Great technology needs great design. We spent over a year refining the hardware — from the sensing mat's thickness (just 9mm) to the base unit's silent operation. The CS-03 was designed to disappear into your bedroom.

Every detail was considered: the mat works under mattresses up to 50cm thick, the base unit connects via a single USB-C cable, and the entire system operates silently with zero LEDs at night.

## The Care Ecosystem Vision

![Indexo app care hub](/images/product/app-care-hub.png)

The Indexo Sleep Monitor is just the beginning. We envision a connected health ecosystem where the sleep mat serves as the central hub — the always-on foundation of your nightly health profile — with additional devices expanding its capabilities over time.

The SpO2 Ring is our first expansion module, adding blood oxygen monitoring for users at risk of sleep-disordered breathing. Looking ahead, we are developing integrations for body temperature tracking, EEG-based sleep staging, and ECG cardiac monitoring.

Our philosophy is simple: **one platform, infinite possibilities.**
